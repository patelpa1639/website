About

I like systems I can take apart — a Kubernetes cluster, an old Miata, a good cup of coffee. Understand it, then make it better.

I'm an engineer in Richmond, Virginia. I studied computer science at Virginia Tech, and these days I lead VMware Cloud Foundation adoption at VMware (Broadcom) — helping big companies modernize the infrastructure their businesses actually run on.

The throughline is curiosity about how complicated things really work. That's what pulled me into founding Sher Systems, an NVIDIA Inception studio where I build AI agents that do real operational work — and it's the same instinct that has me under the hood of a car on the weekend, or elbow-deep in my homelab, which is basically a car project that lives in a rack.

I build because I can't not. There's usually a side project or three going, a lot of coffee involved, and a bias toward shipping the thing over talking about it. I care about systems that are correct, observable, and safe to hand real authority.

The AI brain for infrastructure.
